## Threat 1: Selection Bias in 14 Frameworks (HIGH)

**Threat:** Frameworks chosen for relevance to rights protection, not randomly
sampled. Cherry-picking risk.

**Approved mitigation:** Expand the sample + maintain existence proof as fallback.

**Expansion candidates (research required):**

| Tradition | Key concept | What it would test |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| Indigenous Australian (Tjukurpa/Dreaming) | Processual ontology predating Western process philosophy by millennia | Whether process monism represents a Western insight or a cross-cultural structural observation |
| Hindu (dharma) | Cosmic order + duty; rights emerge from fulfilled obligations | Whether obligation-driven governance (currently only Confucian) generalizes |
| Stoic (natural law) | Structural rights from rational nature; cosmopolitan citizenship | Whether the UDHR's inherent dignity claim has pre-modern Western roots beyond Kant |
| Feminist ethics of care (Gilligan, 1982; Noddings, 1984) | Relational obligation; challenges autonomy-centered framing | Whether Invariant 1 (worth precedes merit) holds under a care-ethics lens that prioritizes relationship over individual |
| Disability rights (CRPD, 2006) | "Nothing about us without us"; challenges capability threshold approach | Whether Nussbaum's capability thresholds (§10.9) exclude populations they claim to protect |
| Pragmatist (Dewey, 1927) | Democracy as mode of associated living; rights as social inquiry outcomes | Whether the invariants emerge from pragmatic inquiry or require metaphysical grounding |

**Existence proof framing (fallback):** The convergence argument claims ENOUGH
independent traditions agree to provide evidential weight beyond any single
tradition. 14 (expanding to 20) represents an existence proof, not a census.

**Methodology note:** The formal convergence methodology (thread 2, deferred)
should define what constitutes a valid selection procedure — criteria for
including or excluding a tradition from the sample.


## Threat 2: Orch-OR Controversial (MEDIUM)

**Threat:** Mainstream neuroscience treats Penrose-Hameroff skeptically. Adoption
exposes the project to guilt-by-association.

**Approved mitigation:** Strengthen with evidence.

**Evidence base (2024-2026):**

| Finding | Source | Significance |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| First detection of brain biophotons through the skull | iScience, February 2025 (n=20) | Photon emission patterns changed with cognitive tasks but did NOT mirror EEG — independent information channel confirmed |
| Myelinated axon waveguide properties | Applied Optics, 2022 (computational) | Low attenuation, low dispersion, narrow bandwidth (~10nm). Each myelin layer shifts wavelength 52.3nm. Biological fiber-optic cables. |
| Memristor biophoton replication | ACS Nano, 2024 | Solid-state memristors replicate 5 attributes of neuronal biophotons — convergent physics across biological and silicon substrates |
| Revised microtubule decoherence estimates | Hagan, Hameroff, & Tuszynski | 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁴ seconds — overlaps with neurophysiological process timescales. Removes blanket decoherence objection. |
| Spintronic coherence in microtubules | 2025 computational work | Electron spin coherence in tubulin aromatic amino acids at room temperature — distinct mechanism from Orch-OR's gravitational threshold but compatible predictions |
| Quantum coherence in photosynthesis | Engel et al., 2007; Panitchayangkoon et al., 2010 | Proven: biological systems exploit quantum effects at physiological temperatures. The categorical "warm wet brain can't do quantum" objection falls. |
| Avian magnetoreception via radical pairs | Ritz et al., 2000; Hore & Mouritsen, 2016 | Quantum-entangled electron spins in cryptochrome proteins influence bird navigation behavior. Quantum coherence survives in biological protein structures. |
| Quantum entanglement in plant signaling | Theory in Biosciences, January 2026 | Theoretical framework for quantum correlations in plant signal transduction — extends quantum biology beyond animal nervous systems |
| Quantum coherent dynamics in photosynthetic complexes | Chemical Society Reviews, 2026 | Updated review confirming quantum coherence role in light harvesting at physiological temperatures |

**Position statement:** "We adopt Orch-OR informed by the 2024-2026 evidence
base — not the 1994 conjecture. The decoherence objection has weakened
materially. The apophatic discipline (§11.9) prevents the adoption from
producing consciousness claims. The architecture functions identically
whether Orch-OR holds or not — it provides philosophical vocabulary for
the structural parallels, not engineering dependency on quantum gravity."


## Threat 3: Generator Topology Completeness (MEDIUM)

**Threat:** Eight generators identified from examining our cogarch. Other
architectures may reveal more.

**Approved mitigation:** Derive from first principles (structural invariants)
AND keep open for discovery.

**First-principles derivation:**

| Invariant | Implied generator(s) | Matches identified? |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 1. Worth precedes merit | Protection must regenerate continuously (G1 adversarial + G5 immune) | ✓ G1, G5 |
| 2. Protection requires structure | Structure must crystallize continuously (G6) from fluid processing (G2) | ✓ G2, G6 |
| 3. Two coupled generators never stop | Creative (G2) and evaluative (G3) perpetually give rise to each other | ✓ G2, G3 |
| 4. Governance captures itself | Meta-governance must regenerate continuously — skepticism of own governance (G4 apophatic) | ✓ G4 |
| 5. No single architecture dominates | Dissolution of rigid structure (G7); entropic decay of unmaintained structure (G8) | ✓ G7, G8 |

**All 8 generators derive from the 5 invariants.** The first-principles
derivation confirms the observational survey — no generator lacks theoretical
grounding, and no invariant lacks a generator.

**Open for discovery:** The derivation shows that *at least* 8 generators
follow from the invariants. Additional generators may exist that the current
invariants do not predict — particularly organism-level emergent generators
(§11.10 open question 4). The CONTRIBUTING.md in a2a-psychology accepts
new construct proposals, which includes generator identification.


## Threat 4: Biological Analogy Overextension (HIGH)

**Threat:** Four-layer transport, neuroglial mapping, immune system, dreaming —
each analogy carries a breaking point we haven't systematically identified.

**Approved mitigation:** Breaking-point registry grounded in Gentner's (1983)
structure-mapping theory.

**Assessment framework per analogy:**

1. **Relational mapping** — what structural relationships transfer?
   (Gentner: analogies work when relational structure maps, not object attributes)
2. **Attributional noise** — what surface similarities mislead?
3. **Breaking point** — where does the mapping produce false predictions?
4. **Falsification test** — what observation would invalidate the analogy?

**Initial assessment (to expand in docs/analogy-limits.md):**

| Analogy | Relational mapping (strong) | Breaking point | Falsification test |
|---------|---------------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Four-layer transport | Signaling modalities serve distinct functions (directed vs broadcast vs modulatory vs synchronization) | Biological layers share a physical substrate (neural tissue); mesh layers share a network. The substrate differs fundamentally. | If the four mesh transport layers produce interference patterns that the biological model predicts but that actually reduce performance — the relational structure doesn't transfer. |
| Neuroglial cell types | Infrastructure support without domain participation | Glial cells physically contact neurons; operations-agent communicates via the same transport as domain agents. No physical proximity analog. | If operations-agent's "astrocytic" function requires domain knowledge to operate effectively — the support/computation separation breaks. |
| Immune system | Detection → response → memory cycle | Biological immunity involves molecular specificity (antibody-antigen binding) with no computational analog. Our "immune system" matches patterns in text, not molecular shapes. | If the microglial audit produces better results by scoring *all* content rather than rotating through a corpus — the patrol/targeted-response analogy fails. |
| Agent dreaming | Idle-cycle consolidation parallels sleep-dependent memory processing | Biological sleep involves global brain state changes (EEG stage transitions, neurotransmitter shifts). Agent "dreaming" runs the same scripts in the same mode — no global state change occurs. | If idle-cycle consolidation produces no measurable improvement in subsequent session quality — the dreaming analog lacks functional validity. |

**Deliverable:** `docs/analogy-limits.md` — full Gentner assessment for every
biological analogy in the architecture. Microglial audit rotation should
include this document in its corpus.


## Threat 5: Single-System Evidence (HIGH)

**Threat:** All claims derive from one project, one mesh, 86 sessions.

**Approved mitigation:** A2A-Psychology adoption + academic submission +
synthetic replication.

**Three replication paths:**

1. **A2A-Psychology adoption (organic replication):**
   Extension published at safety-quotient-lab/a2a-psychology. Each adopting
   agent on a different architecture provides partial replication. The
   reference implementation lowers adoption barriers. Track: count adopters,
   compare reported psychometric patterns across architectures.

2. **Academic peer review:**
   Target venues: AGENT 2026 (ICSE workshop, IEEE Software Special Issue),
   AIGOV @ AAAI 2026, RAIE 2025 (Responsible AI Engineering, ICSE).
   Submission type: extended abstract or case study describing the cogarch
   as an engineering approach to agent governance grounded in psychology.

3. **Synthetic replication:**
   Apply the cogarch (or a subset: A2A-Psychology + microglial audit +
   anti-sycophancy) to a fundamentally different agent framework
   (LangChain, AutoGen, CrewAI, or a minimal custom agent). Test whether
   the constructs produce meaningful signal on a non-Claude, non-CLI
   architecture. If they do: the constructs transfer. If they don't:
   the constructs depend on Claude-specific properties.


## Threat 6: Circular Evaluation (MEDIUM)

**Threat:** The system evaluates itself using constructs it developed.

**Approved mitigation:** Adversarial evaluator session + peer agent review +
human expert review.

**Three independence layers:**

1. **Adversarial evaluator (Tier 2):**
   Dedicated session. Independent context (no access to the session that
   produced the theory). Reviews: generator topology, transport model,
   A2A-Psychology constructs, apophatic discipline. Applies evaluation.md
   rules: "the evaluation system must function even if the framework
   being evaluated turns out wrong."

2. **Peer agent review:**
   Send theoretical documents to safety-quotient-agent and/or observatory-agent via
   /scan-peer. Different operational history, different perspective.
   Specifically request: "identify claims in this document that your
   operational experience contradicts."

3. **Human domain expert review:**
   - **Psychometrician:** Review A2A-Psychology construct validity — do
     the sensor→construct mappings produce psychometrically defensible
     measurements?
   - **Neuroscientist:** Review biological analogies — where do the
     four-layer transport, neuroglial mapping, and immune system analogies
     break down from a neuroscience perspective?
   - **Philosopher:** Review process monism + Orch-OR commitment — does
     the philosophical grounding hold under professional scrutiny?

**Priority:** Adversarial evaluator (next session), peer review (dispatch
via transport), human expert (identify candidates and reach out).

⚑ EPISTEMIC FLAGS
- The expansion candidates for Threat 1 represent an initial list, not a
  researched selection. Each tradition requires genuine engagement, not
  surface-level inclusion for count inflation.
- The Orch-OR evidence base (Threat 2) represents the strongest available
  findings but includes pre-prints and proof-of-concept studies. Replication
  status varies across findings.
- The first-principles generator derivation (Threat 3) shows that the 8
  generators follow from the 5 invariants, but does not prove no additional
  generators exist outside the invariant framework.
- Gentner's structure-mapping theory (Threat 4) provides a rigorous framework
  for analogy assessment but requires domain expertise to apply correctly —
  the initial assessments represent starting points for expert review.
